Computer Parts: The Glorious Guts of Your Beast

Behold the magnificent innards of your soon-to-be digital companion! The heart, the brain, the muscles, and... the, uh, storage thingy. Dive into the fascinating world of computer parts and learn how to pick the perfect organs for your glorious machine! Get Started by clicking a part below!


  • Computer Parts List and Average Pricing
    • CPU
      • A CPU is the brain of a computer, responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations. For gaming, you may consider AMD Ryzen 5 or Ryzen 7, and Intel Core i5 or i7. For work-related tasks, AMD Ryzen 7 or Ryzen 9, and Intel Core i7 or i9 are suitable. Pricing ranges from $100 to $500 for gaming CPUs, while high-end work-oriented CPUs can cost up to $1,000 or more.
    • Motherboard
      • The motherboard is the backbone of a computer, connecting all other components. Consider compatibility with the CPU, RAM, and expansion slots when choosing. Gaming motherboards range from $100 to $300, while work-oriented motherboards can reach up to $500 or more.
    • RAM
      • Random Access Memory (RAM) is essential for multitasking and quick data access. For gaming, 16GB of RAM is recommended, while work-related tasks may require 32GB or more. Pricing ranges from $50 for 8GB to $300+ for high-capacity, high-speed RAM.
    • Storage
      • Storage devices hold your operating system, applications, and files. Solid-state drives (SSDs) offer faster performance than traditional hard disk drives (HDDs). For gaming, a 512GB to 1TB SSD is recommended, while work-related tasks may require 1TB or more. SSD pricing ranges from $50 for 256GB to $200+ for 2TB, while HDDs are generally cheaper.
    • Graphics Card
      • A GPU is responsible for rendering graphics and images. For gaming, NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3060, 3070, or AMD Radeon RX 6700 XT are suitable. For work-related tasks, NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090 or Quadro series, and AMD Radeon RX 6900 XT are options. Gaming GPUs range from $300 to $1,000+, while work-oriented GPUs can cost up to $5,000 or more.
    • Power Supply Unit
      • A PSU supplies power to all components. Choose one with enough wattage to support your components, and consider efficiency ratings (80 PLUS Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, or Titanium). Prices range from $40 for 400W to $200+ for 1200W or more.
    • Cooling
      • Effective cooling is crucial for system stability and longevity. Air cooling is popular and affordable, while liquid cooling offers better performance at a higher cost. Air coolers range from $20 to $100, while liquid cooling kits start at $50 and can reach $300 or more.
    • Accessories
      • Accessories include keyboards, mice, monitors, and more. For gaming, mechanical keyboards, high-DPI mice, and high-refresh-rate monitors are recommended. For work-related tasks, ergonomic keyboards, precise mice, and high-resolution monitors are suitable. Prices vary greatly, from $20 for basic peripherals to $1,000+ for high-end gaming monitors or professional displays.
