Troubleshooting Common Issues - Keeping Your Beast in Check

Even the mightiest of beasts can encounter some bumps along the way. Whether it's a hardware hiccup, software snag, or performance problem, every PC builder will likely face some troubleshooting at one point or another.

Here at CheapBeast, we're here to help you tame your troublesome beast. We'll provide guidance on diagnosing and fixing common issues, ensuring that your PC stays running smoothly and powerfully.

Ready to tackle your PC problems head-on? Let's dive into the world of troubleshooting!


First things first, make sure everything is properly connected. It's like solving a jigsaw puzzle where the pieces can only fit in one way, so double-checking your work is crucial. Ensure your power supply cables are plugged into the right places and your motherboard connectors are snug. Also, don't forget to check that your RAM and GPU are fully seated in their respective slots. You wouldn't want a half-baked cake, would you?


Next, let's address the BIOS, or as some like to call it, the "Big Intelligent Operating System." Just kidding, it's the Basic Input/Output System, but we like to have fun here. It's essentially the software that brings your hardware to life. Sometimes, your PC might not boot because of a BIOS issue, so try resetting it to default settings. If you're still having trouble, you might need to update the BIOS. Don't worry, it's not as scary as it sounds, and it can be a lifesaver. Remember, the BIOS is like the conductor of your PC's orchestra, so keeping it in tune is vital.


Lastly, consider your hardware. Sometimes, a faulty component can bring your whole build crashing down. To identify the culprit, try the process of elimination: remove non-essential parts one by one and test your system each time. If your PC springs to life after removing a certain part, congratulations! You've found the black sheep. Just remember, Rome wasn't built in a day, and neither is a perfect PC. Be patient, stay persistent, and before you know it, you'll be troubleshooting like a pro. And hey, if all else fails, "Have you tried turning it off and on again?"


10 Common PC Building Problems and Solutions

  1. Problem: PC won't power on

    Solution: Check if the power supply is turned on and properly connected to the motherboard.

  2. Problem: No display on the monitor

    Solution: Ensure the monitor is connected to the correct GPU port and that the GPU is properly seated.

  3. Problem: PC turns on, but won't boot

    Solution: Check if RAM is properly seated and compatible with the motherboard.

  4. Problem: Overheating issues

    Solution: Make sure the CPU cooler is properly installed and all fans are functioning correctly.

  5. Problem: USB ports not working

    Solution: Check if the USB headers are connected to the motherboard correctly.

  6. Problem: BIOS not detecting storage drives

    Solution: Check if the storage drives are properly connected to the power supply and motherboard.

  7. Problem: Audio not working

    Solution: Verify that the audio header is connected to the motherboard and the correct audio output is selected in the operating system.

  8. Problem: RAM not detected

    Solution: Ensure the RAM is properly seated in the correct slots and is compatible with your motherboard.

  9. Problem: Graphics card not detected

    Solution: Check if the GPU is properly seated and connected to the power supply, and that the PCIe slot is enabled in BIOS.

  10. Problem: Operating system not installing

    Solution: Verify that your boot device (USB or DVD) is properly connected and the correct boot order is set in BIOS.
